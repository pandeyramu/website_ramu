Organic Chemistry Tips for CEE Nepal

Organic Chemistry carries 17 marks in CEE , tied with Physical Chemistry as the largest Chemistry block. I avoided it for too long. Here is what actually worked when I finally committed to preparing it properly.

By Ramu Pandey Â· Published: March 21, 2026 Â· 9 min read

For the first two months of preparation, I avoided Organic Chemistry. I told myself I would come back to it once the other subjects were stronger. This was a mistake. Organic is worth 17 marks the same as Physical Chemistry and students who skip it or underprepare it are handing those marks to others.

The reason most students avoid it is that it feels overwhelming. Too many reactions, too many reagents, too many conditions. But most of that overwhelm disappears once you stop memorizing reactions in isolation and start understanding them as functional group transformations. That single shift is what made Organic manageable for me.

Think in functional groups, not chapters

The textbook organizes Organic Chemistry by compound class: one chapter for alcohols, one for aldehydes, one for acids. This makes it easy to learn each class in isolation but hard to see how they connect. CEE questions often test those connections what happens when you oxidize a primary alcohol, or what product forms when an alkene undergoes hydration.

I reorganized my notes around functional group transformations instead. A single page showing: alcohol â†’ (mild oxidation) â†’ aldehyde â†’ (strong oxidation) â†’ carboxylic acid â†’ (alcohol + acid) â†’ ester. Once this chain is clear, related questions answer themselves through logic rather than memory.

The distinction test trap: CEE regularly asks which reagent distinguishes between two compounds aldehyde vs ketone, primary vs secondary alcohol, etc. These are reliable marks if you have memorized the tests. Tollens and Fehling react with aldehydes but not ketones. Lucas test distinguishes primary/secondary/tertiary alcohols by reaction speed. Know the result, not just the name of the test.

The timed mini-test on Day 7 matters more than the days of practice leading up to it. Doing 30 questions without time pressure is different from doing 20 questions in 15 minutes. Speed and accuracy under time pressure is what CEE actually tests.

What to revise in the final week before CEE

In the final week, do not open the Organic Chemistry chapter again. Instead, review only three things: your reaction map, your exception sheet, and your personal error log of Organic mistakes. These three documents contain exactly the information you need and nothing extra.

If there are distinction tests you are still unsure about Tollens vs Fehling, Lucas test conditions write them on one card and keep it in your revision kit for the last few days. These are easy marks that require only exact memory, and they are worth the targeted effort.
