Chapter-wise Marks Distribution for CEE: What to Prioritise

Not all chapters are equally worth your time. Here is the actual marks breakdown across Biology, Chemistry, Physics, and MAT and how I used it to stop wasting study hours on low-return areas.

By Ramu Pandey · Published: March 21, 2026 · 8 min read

One of the best decisions I made during CEE preparation was sitting down and mapping out exactly how many marks each chapter was worth before I started studying. Before that, I was studying chapters in the order they appeared in the textbook, which meant some low-weightage chapters were getting the same attention as ones worth three times the marks.

The table below shows the official CEE question distribution. Use it as your study priority guide, not just a reference.

How I used this to plan my study time

Once I saw the distribution, I built a rough rule: for every 10 marks a chapter was worth, I gave it one focused week of preparation before moving to the consolidation phase. Low-weightage chapters (2–3 marks) got a single focused day, not a week.

I also tracked my chapter-wise accuracy in MCQ practice and compared it against weightage. A chapter where I was scoring 50% accuracy but worth 15 marks was my highest priority. A chapter where I was scoring 80% but worth 3 marks was something I could leave for quick revision later.

  • High weightage + low accuracy = immediate focus
  • High weightage + decent accuracy = maintain with regular MCQ drills
  • Low weightage + low accuracy = quick targeted revision, not a deep dive
  • Low weightage + high accuracy = light revision only, do not over-invest
