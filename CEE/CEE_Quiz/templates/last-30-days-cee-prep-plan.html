Last 30 Days CEE Preparation Plan: Time-sensitive, High-Impact

The final 30 days are not about learning new things. They are about converting what you already know into exam marks. Here is exactly how to use that time.

By Ramu Pandey · Published: March 21, 2026 · 7 min read

There is a particular kind of panic that hits in the last month before CEE. You feel like you have not covered enough, that other students are ahead, and that you need to somehow read everything again from scratch. I felt this too. The students who handle this phase well are not the ones who study the hardest, they are the ones who stop adding new content and start consolidating what they already have.

The last 30 days should be split into three 10-day cycles: quick revision and gap-patching, intensive MCQ drilling, and full mock test simulation. Here is how each phase works.

Days 1–10: Patch your gaps, not your confidence

Use the first 10 days to identify and fix the topics you keep getting wrong, not to re-read everything. Build a short error sheet: a list of concepts, formulas, and chapter areas where you lose marks repeatedly. Revise those specifically. Avoid picking up new chapters you skipped entirely; if you have not touched them in months, this is not the time.

Daily TaskTime
Targeted revision of weak chapters45 min
Chapter-wise MCQ practice30 min
Update error sheet with new mistakes15 min

I kept my error sheet in a small notebook, one line per mistake: chapter, the wrong answer I chose, and why. Reviewing this before bed took five minutes but made the next day's revision far more targeted.

Days 11–20: Stop reading, start drilling

In this phase, close the textbooks. Every hour should go toward MCQ practice , first chapter-wise in the morning, then mixed timed sets in the evening. The goal is to stop relying on recognition ("I think I know this") and build actual recall under time pressure.

Daily TaskTime
Focused chapter MCQ sets (morning)45 min
Mixed timed MCQ set (evening)45 min
Error sheet review and re-attempt20 min

Re-attempt your error sheet questions every 48 hours. If you are still getting the same ones wrong after three attempts, that is the concept to go back and actually re-read , not before.

Key rule for days 11–20: If you catch yourself reading a chapter instead of solving questions, stop. Reading feels productive but MCQ drilling is what actually moves your score in this phase.

Days 21–30: Simulate the real exam

At least three full-length mock tests in the final 10 days. Same time of day as your CEE slot. No phone. Strict 180-minute limit. After each test, spend as long reviewing wrong answers as you spent taking the test, this is not optional. The review is where improvement actually happens.

  • Test 1 (Day 21) → identify pacing problems and weak sections
  • Test 2 (Day 25) → focus on accuracy improvements from Test 1 review
  • Test 3 (Day 28 or 29) → simulate complete exam conditions, final calibration

Final 48 hours: less is more

Do not try to cram new content in the last two days. Your brain needs time to consolidate, not to absorb more. Use the final 48 hours only for: your formula list, your reaction map, your error sheet, and a calm night of sleep. Pack everything you need for exam day the evening before.

  • Pack admit card, documents, and stationery the night before
  • No new chapters in the final 48 hours
  • Sleep on time , calculation speed drops significantly with poor sleep
