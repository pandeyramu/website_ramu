About CEE MCQ

CEE MCQ started from a simple frustration. While preparing for Nepal's Common Entrance Examination, finding good chapter-wise practice questions online was harder than it should have been. Notes were scattered across PDFs, Facebook groups, and outdated blog posts, and there was no single place to actually test what you knew, chapter by chapter, the way the real exam would test you. This site was built to fix that gap.

What CEE MCQ Actually Does

CEE MCQ is a free practice platform for students preparing for the CEE in Biology, Chemistry, Physics, and MAT. Instead of long reading sessions, the site is built around active practice: chapter-wise quizzes, subchapter-level drills for topics that need closer attention, and full-length mock tests that simulate the real 180-question exam with negative marking and a timer. Every quiz tracks your score, accuracy, and time taken, so you can see exactly where you are improving and where you are not.

Why Chapter-Wise Practice Matters

Not every chapter in the CEE syllabus carries equal weight, and not every student has equal time. CEE MCQ is organized so students can drill specific weak chapters instead of re-reading entire subjects. This matters most in the final months before the exam, when targeted revision produces better results than broad, unfocused study. The platform's structure reflects that priority: subject pages break down into chapters, and chapters break down into subchapters where the syllabus calls for it.

Who Built This

CEE MCQ is built and maintained by Ramu Pandey, who created the platform after going through CEE preparation and noticing how disorganized the available practice resources were. The site is a solo, ongoing project rather than a large commercial product, which means it stays focused on what actually helps students revise rather than on unrelated features. Feedback from real students using the platform directly shapes what gets added or changed.

Beyond Practice Questions

Alongside the quizzes, the site includes a blog with practical study guides covering topics like chapter-wise marks distribution, organic chemistry reaction maps, MAT strategy, and exam-day planning. These are written from direct experience with CEE preparation, not generic study advice repeated across the internet. The intent is for a student to be able to land on the site, understand exactly where they stand in a chapter, and know what to do next.

Where This Is Going

CEE MCQ is actively maintained, and new chapters, subchapters, and blog content are added as the question bank grows. The long-term goal is to make this the most complete free chapter-wise CEE practice resource available to Nepali students, without paywalls or unnecessary sign-up barriers getting in the way of practice.

Questions or Feedback

If you spot an incorrect question, have a suggestion for a missing chapter, or just want to share how your preparation is going, you can reach out through the contact page. Most improvements to this platform have come directly from students pointing out what was missing.
