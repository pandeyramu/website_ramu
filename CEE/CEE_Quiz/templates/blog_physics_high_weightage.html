High Weightage Physics Topics for CEE Nepal

Physics carries 50 marks in CEE and Modern Physics alone is worth 12, more than any other single unit in the Physics section. Here is how I prioritized each chapter and what I would do differently if I started again.

By Ramu Pandey Â· Published: March 21, 2026 Â· 10 min read

Physics was the subject I was most uncertain about going into CEE preparation. It felt formula-heavy and I had always been slower at numericals than at Biology recall. What changed my approach was realizing that most CEE Physics questions test a small set of formulas applied in predictable ways and that knowing which chapters to prioritize made a much bigger difference than trying to master everything equally.

The chapter that surprised me most was Modern Physics. It carries 12 marks , the single highest unit in Physics and yet many students underprepare it because it feels abstract. Once I focused on it properly, it became one of my more reliable scoring areas.

Mechanics â†’ 10 marks, the foundation chapter

Mechanics was actually one of my stronger areas because the concepts build on each other logically. Newton's laws lead naturally to work and energy, which leads to circular motion and gravitation. If you understand the logic rather than memorizing each formula in isolation, recall under time pressure becomes easier.

The question types I saw most in practice tests were projectile motion numericals, work-energy theorem applications, and circular motion problems. These three are worth drilling specifically.

This chapter rewards formula practice more than any other in Physics. Circuit questions, Kirchhoff's laws, and electromagnetic induction are predictable question types, if you have practiced them with timed numericals, they feel routine in the exam.

The one habit that improved my Physics score most: Keeping a formula notebook with every formula, its SI units, and the context in which it applies. I reviewed this notebook for 10 minutes before every Physics practice session. Forgetting a formula in a Physics numerical is a costly mistake â€” the notebook made it much rarer.

How I structured Physics preparation week by week

I started with Modern Physics despite it feeling unfamiliar, because the marks-to-effort ratio is the best in the Physics section. Once that was stable, I moved to Mechanics, then Current Electricity. Waves and Optics and Thermodynamics came next, and Electrostatics last.

  • Two days concept revision per chapter, with formula memorization
  • Three days of chapter-wise MCQ drills , 30 questions minimum per session
  • One mixed Physics test per week under time pressure
  • Error log updated after every session, reviewed before the next

The weekly mixed test was what moved my Physics score the most. Drilling one chapter at a time builds knowledge but not speed across topics. Mixed tests build the kind of flexible recall that CEE actually requires.
