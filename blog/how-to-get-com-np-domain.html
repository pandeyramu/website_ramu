How to Get a .com.np Domain in Nepal, Step by Step

By Ramu Pandey Published May 2026 5 min read

Registering a .com.np domain is a great choice for Nepal-based individuals and organisations. This guide walks you through the full process including eligibility, documents, choosing a registrar, configuring DNS, and common pitfalls to avoid.

Eligibility

.com.np domains are available to Nepali citizens or organisations. The rules may differ slightly between registrars, but generally you need to provide identification such as a citizen ID or company registration and a local contact point. If you lack Nepali ID, consider using a local partner or a commercial registrar that supports non-resident applicants, and verify their terms carefully before proceeding.

Choose a registrar

Several Nepalese registrars and resellers handle .com.np domain registrations. Compare prices, support quality, and DNS management features before deciding. Many registrars offer bundled hosting and email which can simplify setup for beginners. Read the registrar's documentation about verification and renewal policies before you start the process.

Required documents and verification

Prepare these documents before initiating registration. Most registrars will ask you to upload scanned copies during signup, so ensure scans are clear and legible to avoid verification delays.

  • Copy of your Nepali citizenship ID or company registration certificate.
  • Contact details including email, phone, and postal address.
  • Proof of ownership if registering on behalf of an organisation such as a business license.

Step-by-step registration

  1. Search availability and pick a short, memorable domain matching your brand.
  2. Create an account with your chosen registrar and provide contact details.
  3. Upload required documents for verification and wait for approval, which usually takes a few hours to a few days.
  4. Once approved, set up DNS records to point to your hosting provider.
  5. Enable HTTPS by obtaining an SSL certificate. Let's Encrypt is free and automated.
  6. Verify the domain resolves correctly and configure email if needed.

DNS setup basics

After registration, configure DNS records using your registrar's control panel or your hosting DNS provider. At minimum you need an A record to point the root domain to your server IP, a CNAME to point www to the root, and MX records if you need email. Allow DNS propagation time of usually a few minutes to a few hours, then test using online DNS lookup tools.

SSL and HTTPS

Always enable HTTPS on your site. Use Let's Encrypt for a free certificate or purchase one if you need extended validation. Most managed hosting providers support automatic certificate issuance, so enabling HTTPS is often a single checkbox in the control panel. Skipping HTTPS causes browser warnings and affects search rankings.

Costs and renewal

Registrar prices vary but expect a small annual fee for registration and renewal. Additional charges may apply for privacy protection, hosting, or email. Keep track of renewal dates to avoid domain expiry, enable auto-renew where available, and ensure your payment method stays up to date.

Common pitfalls

  • Low-quality scans cause verification delays, so always upload clear documents.
  • Using a third-party reseller without clear ownership records can create transfer problems later. Ensure the registrant name is correct from the start.
  • Failing to enable HTTPS causes user trust issues and search ranking penalties that are easy to avoid.

Final checklist

Before going live, confirm that DNS resolves correctly, HTTPS is active, your contact email works, and WHOIS shows the correct registrant details. With these in place your .com.np domain will be ready to support your site and build local trust with Nepali users and search engines.
