How to Get a .com.np Domain in Nepal, Step by Step

By Ramu Pandey · Published: 2026-05-23 · 5 min read

Registering a .com.np domain is a great choice for Nepal-based individuals and organisations. This guide walks you through the full process, including eligibility, documents, choosing a registrar, configuring DNS, and common pitfalls to avoid. If you want a country-specific domain that signals Nepal relevance, a .com.np is an excellent option.

Eligibility

.com.np domains are typically available to Nepali citizens or organisations. The rules may differ slightly between registrars, but generally you need to provide identification (citizen ID or company registration) and a local contact point. If you lack Nepali ID, consider using a local partner or a commercial registrar that offers support for non-resident applicants (be cautious and verify terms).

Choose a registrar

There are several Nepalese registrars and resellers that handle .com.np domain registrations. Compare prices, support, and DNS management features. Many registrars offer bundled hosting and email, which can simplify setup for beginners. Read the registrar’s documentation about verification and renewal policies before proceeding.

Required documents and verification

Prepare these documents before initiating registration:

  • Copy of your Nepali citizenship ID or company registration certificate.
  • Contact details: email, phone, and postal address.
  • Proof of ownership if registering on behalf of an organisation (e.g., business license).

Most registrars will ask you to upload scanned copies during the signup process. Ensure scans are clear and legible to avoid verification delays.

Step-by-step registration

  1. Search availability: pick a short, memorable domain matching your brand.
  2. Create an account with your chosen registrar and provide contact details.
  3. Upload required documents for verification and wait for approval (often a few hours to a few days).
  4. Once approved, set up DNS records (A, CNAME, MX) to point to your hosting provider.
  5. Enable HTTPS by obtaining an SSL/TLS certificate (Let’s Encrypt is free and automated).
  6. Verify the domain resolves correctly and configure email if needed.

DNS setup basics

After registration, configure DNS records using your registrar’s control panel or your hosting DNS provider. At minimum:

  • A record: point the root domain to your server IP.
  • CNAME: point `www` to the root (or set `www` A record).
  • MX: configure mail servers if you need email.

Allow DNS propagation time (usually minutes to a few hours) and test using `dig` or online tools.

SSL and HTTPS

Always enable HTTPS. Use Let’s Encrypt for a free certificate or purchase one if you need extended validation. Most providers support automatic certificate issuance, so enabling HTTPS is often a single checkbox in managed hosting panels.

Costs and renewal

Registrar prices vary. Expect a small annual fee for registration and renewal; additional charges apply for privacy, hosting, or email. Keep track of renewal dates to avoid domain expiry; enable auto-renew where available and ensure payment methods are up to date.

Common pitfalls

  • Low-quality scans cause verification delays, so upload clear documents.
  • Using a third-party reseller without clear ownership can create transfer problems later; ensure the registrant name is correct.
  • Failing to enable HTTPS causes user trust issues and search ranking penalties.

Final checklist

Before going live, confirm: DNS resolves, HTTPS is active, contact email works, and WHOIS shows correct registrant details. With these in place, your .com.np domain will be ready to support your site and local trust signals.
