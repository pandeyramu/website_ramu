How to Prepare for CEE Effectively

By Ramu Pandey · Published: 2026-05-23 · 8 min read

This guide lays out a practical plan to prepare for the Common Entrance Examination (CEE) in Nepal. It covers a realistic study schedule, subject prioritization by weightage, the importance of MCQ practice, and a short checklist you can follow to track progress.

Study schedule and planning

Start with a clear timeline. A typical effective preparation plan spans 8–12 weeks, split into three phases: foundation, consolidation, and simulation. In the foundation phase you rebuild or reinforce core theory; in consolidation you switch to chapter-wise MCQ practice and review; in simulation you do full-length tests under timed conditions. Each week should have defined goals: concept completion, targeted MCQ sets, and a small mixed timed practice session.

Weekly breakdown

  • Weeks 1–4: Finish core chapters and do short chapter quizzes.
  • Weeks 5–8: Increase MCQ volume, do mixed drills and error analysis.
  • Weeks 9–12: Full-length mock tests and focused revision of weak topics.

Subject-wise priority (Biology, Chemistry, Physics, MAT)

Not all subjects carry equal weight. In CEE, Biology (often split into Botany and Zoology combined) tends to have the largest share, followed by Chemistry and Physics; MAT is shorter but fast to improve. Prioritise high-weightage chapters first because they give quick score gains, but do not completely ignore low-weightage chapters.

Biology

Focus on human physiology, cell biology, genetics and commonly tested diagrams. Use short summary notes and flashcards to reinforce terminology and processes.

Chemistry

Split time between physical chemistry practice (equations and numericals), organic reaction patterns, and quick inorganic facts. Reaction maps help reduce rote memorization and speed up answers.

Physics

Begin with mechanics and modern physics topics that often repeat in exams. Diagram-based questions and unit handling are frequent error sources, so practice solving with clear steps.

MAT

Practice short daily timed drills. MAT rewards pattern recognition and speed, and moderate daily practice yields large improvements quickly.

Importance of MCQ practice

MCQ practice does more than evaluate you; it helps build pattern recognition, time management, and error analysis skills. Make a habit of keeping an error log: label mistakes as concept gaps, careless errors, or time-pressure mistakes and re-test them after 3 and 7 days.

Link to online practice

Use chapter-wise MCQ practice for targeted improvement. You can practise at ceemcq.pandeyramu.com.np which hosts thousands of chapter-wise questions and full mock tests.

Reading time and pacing

Set micro-targets each session: 45–60 minutes focused study, 10–15 minutes quick revision, and 30–45 minutes of MCQs. Keep timed mini-tests weekly to measure progress and adjust the plan.

Final CEE exam checklist

  • Make a weekly study plan with fixed time blocks for Biology, Chemistry, Physics, and MAT.
  • Revise high-weightage chapters first, then cover smaller topics you often forget.
  • Keep an error log for wrong MCQs and revisit it before every test.
  • Practice timed MCQs every week to improve speed and accuracy.
  • Take at least one full CEE mock test each week during the simulation phase.

Goal: build exam confidence through steady revision, repeated MCQ practice, and full-test timing so you enter CEE prepared for both content and speed.
