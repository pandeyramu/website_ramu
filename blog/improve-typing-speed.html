How to Improve Typing Speed: Tools and Techniques

By Ramu Pandey · Published: 2026-05-23 · 6 min read

Typing speed is a valuable skill for students and developers alike. Faster, accurate typing reduces friction between ideas and implementation, improves productivity, and is especially helpful when taking timed online tests.

Why typing speed matters

For students, being able to type quickly helps when taking online practice tests, writing answers in limited time, or taking notes during lectures. For developers, typing speed speeds up coding, debugging, and documentation tasks.

How to use the Typing Speed Test on this site

Visit the Typing Speed Test on this site. The test shows a timed passage and measures accuracy and words-per-minute (WPM). Use short sessions daily and track progress in localStorage or a notebook.

Practice techniques

  1. Start slow: focus on accuracy before speed.
  2. Use home row positioning and practise common letter sequences.
  3. Do short, frequent drills (5–10 minutes) rather than long sessions.
  4. Track both WPM and accuracy; improving one at the expense of the other is not helpful.

Recommended tools

Several online resources and simple drills help you improve: typing tutorials, random-word drills, and copy-typing passages. Keep a weekly log of WPM and accuracy to spot plateaus and plan interventions.

Long-term habit

Make typing practice a daily habit. Replace one passive activity (like social media scrolling) with a focused 10-minute typing drill. Small daily improvements compound quickly.
